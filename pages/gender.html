Gender

Theory

Performativity

Gender is not simply something one is, but something repeatedly done, enacted, and recognized through social rituals, expectations, and norms. This concept helps explain how identity is produced through repetition rather than revealed as a stable inner truth.

Judith Butler
Philosopher and gender theorist
“Gender is the repeated stylization of the body.”

Concept

Heteronormativity

Heteronormativity describes the social system that presents heterosexuality, gender binarism, and conventional family structures as natural, universal, and desirable. It organizes institutions, law, media, and everyday life.

Michael Warner
Literary and queer theorist
A norm can feel invisible precisely because it is treated as natural.

Debate

Biology vs Identity

This tension appears whenever gender is reduced to anatomy, chromosomes, or reproductive capacity. Critical approaches ask what biology can explain, what it cannot, and how “nature” is often used as a political argument rather than a neutral fact.

Anne Fausto-Sterling
Biologist and feminist scholar
Bodies matter, but they never speak outside culture.

History

Brief History of Gender as a Social Category

Gender moved from a taken-for-granted social role to an object of critique, political struggle, and academic analysis. Feminism, queer theory, trans thought, and postcolonial critique all changed how gender could be named, studied, resisted, and lived.

Simone de Beauvoir
Philosopher and feminist writer
“One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman.”

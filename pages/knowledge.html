Core concept

Epistemology

Epistemology is the study of knowledge: what counts as knowledge, how we know what we know, who is treated as credible, and which methods are considered valid. In queer and critical thought, epistemology matters because knowledge is never neutral. It is shaped by institutions, norms, access, and power.

Michel Foucault
Philosopher of knowledge and power
Knowledge does not stand outside power. It is one of the ways power works.

Lens

Situated Knowledge

Situated knowledge challenges the fantasy of a detached, universal point of view. It argues that all knowledge comes from somewhere: from a body, a history, a location, a social position. Instead of pretending to be nowhere, critical knowledge asks us to be accountable for where we speak from.

Donna Haraway
Scholar of science, embodiment, and situated knowledge
Objectivity is not the view from nowhere, but a responsible account of partial perspective.

Challenge

Cognitive Bias

Cognitive bias refers to the recurring patterns through which perception and judgment can become distorted. We do not meet facts as blank slates. We interpret them through expectation, fear, familiarity, ideology, and social training. Bias is not just an individual flaw. It can also be built into institutions, research traditions, and public common sense.

Daniel Kahneman
Psychologist of judgment and decision-making
We tend to mistake familiar narratives for truth, even when they narrow what we can perceive.

Why it matters

Who gets to count as knowledge?

These ideas belong together because they expose a central question: whose voice is treated as credible, objective, educated, rational, or universal? Knowledge is not only discovered. It is sorted, ranked, institutionalized, and defended. Critical theory helps us see how some experiences become evidence while others are dismissed as emotion, anecdote, identity, or noise.

bell hooks
Writer and critic of knowledge, education, and power
Theory matters when it helps us name what has been lived but not yet recognized as knowledge.

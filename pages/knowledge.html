Knowledge

This page explores key concepts in knowledge, including Epistemology, Situated Knowledge, Cognitive Biases, and Queer as a Method of Learning. These concepts are connected by their focus on how we learn, process information, and how our perspectives shape knowledge.

Epistemology

Epistemology is the study of knowledge, including its nature, origin, and limits. It investigates questions such as "What is knowledge?" and "How is knowledge different from belief or opinion?"

Immanuel Kant

"Science is organized knowledge. Wisdom is organized life."

Situated Knowledge

Situated knowledge emphasizes that all knowledge is influenced by our position in society and the contexts in which we are situated. It challenges the idea of neutral or objective knowledge.

Donna Haraway

"Knowledge is always situated, influenced by our experiences, our identities, and our perspectives."

Cognitive Biases

Cognitive biases are systematic patterns of deviation from norm or rationality in judgment. These biases affect how we perceive and make decisions.

Daniel Kahneman

"We are all subject to biases, but recognizing them allows us to make better decisions."

QUEER as a Method of Learning

Queer theory challenges conventional understandings of gender, sexuality, and identity. It offers a unique method of learning that disrupts traditional approaches and encourages critical thinking.

Judith Butler

"Gender is not something we are, but something we do."
